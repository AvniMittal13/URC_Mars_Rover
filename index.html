About

Our Vision and Purpose

Team Deimos is a group of young, enthusiastic people who are designing and building high-tech Mars rovers. As members of SNTC IIT Mandi and Robotronics Club, IIT Mandi, we aspire to make our thoughts a reality by producing and hard at work on our projects. Our prototypes compete in a number of international and national competitions. The projects are intended to be a teaching and learning experience for those interested in mechanical engineering, software, electronics, or robotics and exposes us to a variety of other engineering fields, both practical and theoretical, broadening our expertise. As a group, we learn to grasp industry work cultures and problem-solving in real-time, all while adhering to a planned deadline.

Know about the tech-stack and other specifications of our ROver.

  • Mechanical

    Aluminium Chassis Design, 6 Wheel drive and Robotic Arm to perform mechanical work.

  • Electronics and Communication

    Power, Controls, remote communication and navigations with help of cameras, antennas, sensors, etc.

    Life-detection module that will provide on-site detection of life forms on Planet Mars. Identifying Biosignatures, identification of Lipids and proteins and mineral identification.

    Nvidia Jetson nano as the main computer with two raspberry pi for the rover.

RORAS

Remotely Operated Robotic Arm.

The project RORAS ( Remotely Operated Robotic Arm System ) is a system which imitates the movement of hands of a master controller and does the same actions using a robotic arm kept at some distance. The system consists of a robotic arm which can be remotely controlled from a distance by a person and imitate the hand movements of the person. On the controlling side, dubbed “master” side, a person may enact the procedure to be done while holding two probes inside a box and moving them accordingly. These movements are picked up by the system through cameras and the same actions or gestures are followed by the robotic arms which are kept at some remote location. The data is transferred over the internet.

Autonomous Drone

Self - Driving Drone Swarm

The project is a UAV fleet that has to find 4 out of 5 mannequins in the field of 40m X 40m. In this task, we are developing a fleet of three quadcopters that relies on master-slave Technology. In this set-up one drone will control the other two drones serving as their communication hub. We will be using openCV for image processing that will be used for object identification.
